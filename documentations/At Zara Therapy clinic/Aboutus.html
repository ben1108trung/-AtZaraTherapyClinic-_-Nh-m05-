What is Physiotherapy?

Physiotherapy is a clinical specialty of medicine, applying techniques based on physical, physiological, and psychological factors ... acting directly or indirectly on the body to cause stimulation. and treat some diseases.

With special advantages, treating the disease without drugs, VLTL has quickly gained the upper hand. Due to the side effects of drugs or chemotherapy methods, there is an increased demand for natural remedies such as physical therapy. Many patients also hope to use physical measures to treat or avoid surgery.

History of Physiotherapy

Physiotherapy was born in 2500 BC (BC) in China, Hippocrates described massage and hydrotherapy in 460 BC and can be said to be a version of modern physical therapy methods today. Initially, Physiotherapy originated from massage to relieve fatigue, simple exercises to limit functional decline brought remarkable results. When scientific technology develops, physical therapy is no longer just simple exercise movements, people have learned to apply science and technology to physical therapy, and medical devices have begun to appear.

These devices are increasingly improved and constantly improve their features and uses. Among the three orthodox medical treatments including surgery, chemotherapy and physical therapy, Physiotherapy has a long history and ups and downs. Thousands of years BC some physical agents such as heat, cold, magnetic field of natural magnet stone, ... have been used to cure diseases. Until the end of the 17th century they were still considered the basic therapies of medicine (Beckeer, 1990).
