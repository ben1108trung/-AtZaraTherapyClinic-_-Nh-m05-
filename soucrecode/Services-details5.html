Osteopathy

Osteopathy Guide

Manual Osteopathy is a type of manual therapy (meaning we use our hands to assess as well as treat) that aims to restore optimal function in the body. This hand skill allows the practitioner to feel not only surface tensions, like muscle tensions and inflammation but also deeper tensions and distortions. In addition to techniques for muscles, joints, and the nervous system, there are many other tools and techniques that manual osteopaths use to work with the musculoskeletal system, as well as the visceral system.

Restricted list

The following is a limited list of conditions that may respond to manual osteopathic treatment: musculoskeletal complaints such as back, shoulder, arm, hip, leg pain concussions Digestive system complaints such as constipation, and acid reflux) Anxiety and depression Fatigue Symptoms from falls or accidents such as whiplash injuries Symptoms related to chronic conditions such as Shortness of breath due to asthma) Manual osteopathy can complement other therapeutic approaches like Physiotherapy, massage therapy, chiropractic, and acupuncture very well. In the treatment of certain conditions (ie. depression, anxiety, some chronic conditions), manual osteopaths may also make referrals to other health care practitioners like naturopathic doctors, MD’s or psychologists for additional necessary support.
